CONCLUSIONS & RECOMMENDATIONS
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

✓✓✓ SYMMETRIC SCHEDULER (RECOMMENDED FOR PRODUCTION)

  Strengths:
    • 83.5% lower jitter (0.04µs vs 0.23µs default)
    • 89% lower maximum latency (7.50µs vs 66.94µs)
    • 75% better P99 tail latency (0.04µs vs 0.15µs)
    • Thread-local detection maximizes cache locality
    • Reactive work-stealing avoids probing overhead
  Best For: Time-sensitive edge workloads with competing applications
  Recommendation: DEPLOY TO PRODUCTION

✓ DEDICATED SCHEDULER (RCFS) (FALLBACK OPTION)

  Strengths:
    • O(1) atomic deficit tracking prevents starvation
    • 46% lower jitter (0.12µs vs 0.23µs default)
    • 80% lower maximum latency (13.54µs vs 66.94µs)
    • Predictable behavior under load
  Best For: Fair scheduling with moderate performance requirements  
  Recommendation: Use if Symmetric shows instability in production

✓ DEFAULT SCHEDULER (BASELINE - ACCEPTABLE)

  Strengths:
    • Simplicity and low overhead
    • Predictable baseline behavior
  Weaknesses:
    • High jitter and maximum latency
    • Vulnerable to head-of-line blocking
  Best For: Non-critical workloads, baseline comparisons only
  Recommendation: Consider upgrading to RCFS or Symmetric
